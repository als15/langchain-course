# Mila Design System

## Brand Identity

**Brand Name:** Mila
**Tagline:** גלידה קצת איטלקית, הרבה ישראלית
**Brand Voice:** Warm, playful, polished, and local. Mila should feel boutique but never stiff: cheerful, design-aware, family-friendly, a little Italian and very Israeli.
**Visual Style:** Clean white base, airy pastel palette, hand-drawn line-art cone icon, modern minimal typography, soft diffused studio lighting with minimal shadows, and a neighborhood weekend feel.

---

## Color System

### Primary Palette

```text
Primary     #1B3A5C    — Brand anchor for digital headers, buttons, and structure
Secondary   #F97316    — Warm accent for calls to action, highlights, and promos
Background  #FFFFFF    — Clean bright canvas
Text        #0F2238    — Primary text / strong contrast
```

### Supporting Brand Colors

```text
Sky Blue    #DBEDF9    — Airy pastel support, scoop color, soft backgrounds
Cream       #EEE5AD    — Soft warmth, scoop color, subtle highlight blocks
Blush       #F4D3D3    — Friendly, feminine-leaning warmth without feeling childish
Cone        #F1D0AA    — Natural cone tone, food warmth, grounding neutral
Peach       #FDBA74    — Light accent for promos, stickers, and playful details
```

### Color Usage Notes

- Keep large surfaces white or very light to preserve the clean truck-and-packaging feel.
- Use navy for structure, typography, menus, and digital legibility.
- Use orange sparingly for emphasis, promos, buttons, or seasonal announcements.
- Use pastels in pairs or trios, not all at full intensity at once.
- The black line-art cone from the logo can be used as a graphic accent when navy feels too heavy.

## Typography

### Font Stack

```css
/* Titles / Headlines */
font-family: 'Avenir Next', 'Montserrat', 'Helvetica Neue', sans-serif;

/* Body / Captions */
font-family: 'Inter', 'Heebo', 'Arial', sans-serif;

/* Optional accent script for occasional seasonal callouts only */
font-family: 'Dancing Script', cursive;
```

### Typography Direction

- Headlines should feel spacious, clean, geometric, and modern.
- Body copy should stay simple, warm, and highly legible on mobile.
- Wide letter spacing can be used sparingly to echo the logo wordmark.
- Accent script should be rare and decorative, never the primary reading experience.
- Avoid retro carnival fonts, cartoon lettering, or overly condensed display fonts.

## Visual Language

- **Photography style:** Bright natural daylight, premium product close-ups, shallow depth of field, soft summer shadows, cheerful candid moments, and real human presence around the truck. Aesthetic minimalism: favor solid pastel backgrounds over busy outdoor scenes. High-key, clean, and graphic.
- **Composition preferences:** Hero product centered or slightly off-center; truck branding, line-art logo, and pastel pattern used as backdrop; vertical-first framing for Stories and Reels; clean negative space for copy overlays.Centric framing: the product should be dead-center. Use a 'flat' perspective where the background feels like a solid wall of color.
- **Color treatment:** Crisp whites, true food color, warm skin tones, pastel blocks, soft contrast, and no heavy filters.
- **Brand motifs:** Continuous line-art cone, overlapping pastel scoops, sprinkle shapes, waffle cone texture, white truck surfaces, and an easy neighborhood weekend atmosphere.
- **Things to avoid visually:** Dark moody edits, neon colors, cluttered props, fake readable text on cups or truck surfaces, excessive melting, messy drips, overly posed stock-photo energy, and nightlife or luxury-hotel styling that feels off-brand.Avoid heavy bokeh or overly blurred street backgrounds; avoid harsh direct sunlight with dark shadows.
