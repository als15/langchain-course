# Capa & Co — Video Generation Prompt

A smooth, slow-motion cinematic brand video for a boutique content studio. Warm cream (#FAF7F2) and deep olive green (#4A5D3A) color palette throughout. The scene opens on a matte ceramic coffee cup on a linen-textured surface, bathed in soft golden-hour light. Camera slowly glides across a minimal workspace — dark charcoal surfaces (#2A2A26) contrasting with warm off-white elements. Sage green (#8FA878) botanical accents, dried olive branches, and natural earth tones fill the frame. Typography appears in elegant Hebrew serif font (Frank Ruhl Libre style) with generous letter-spacing, floating gently over cream backgrounds. Subtle UI elements — rounded cards with hairline borders, soft status badges in muted gold and blue — fade in and out like a living dashboard. The mood is professional yet warm, organic yet precise — like an editorial atelier. Shallow depth of field, film grain, 24fps, muted tonal range with no harsh whites or saturated colors. Think Kinfolk magazine meets a modern SaaS dashboard. Aspect ratio 16:9.

## Caprese Focaccia — Baking Video Prompt

Overhead slow-motion shot of hands pressing dimples into soft, pillowy focaccia dough on a flour-dusted wooden surface. Extra virgin olive oil pools into each dimple, glistening under warm natural light. The camera slowly pulls back as fingers arrange ripe cherry tomatoes — deep red, sun-warmed — pressing them gently into the dough. Thick slices of fresh mozzarella are laid between the tomatoes, their milky white surface catching the light. Bright green fresh basil leaves are scattered on top, still dewy, one leaf falling in slow motion. A generous drizzle of olive oil cascades from above in a golden thread. Cut to the focaccia sliding into a hot oven — a rush of warm air, dough beginning to rise. Time-lapse of the bread baking: edges turning golden, cheese melting and bubbling, tomatoes blistering and splitting. The finished focaccia emerges — golden crust, caramelized edges, molten mozzarella stretching as a slice is pulled away. Close-up of the cross-section: airy crumb, pockets of olive oil, melted cheese pulling in strings. A hand tears a piece and lifts it — steam rising, fresh basil on top. Final frame: the whole focaccia on a linen cloth, sprinkled with flaky sea salt and cracked pepper, a sprig of basil beside it, bathed in golden-hour side light. Shallow depth of field, warm cream and olive green tones, muted earth palette, film grain, 24fps. The vibe is fresh, handmade, Mediterranean morning — bread just out of the oven, kitchen still warm.
